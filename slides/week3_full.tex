\section*{Introduction to Matrix Transformations}
- Matrices as transformations in space:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Reflections
	\item Rotations
	\item Scaling
	\item Shearing
\end{itemize}

\section*{Reflection}
- Definition: Matrix that flips vectors across a line/plane.
- Example:
\[
	\begin{bmatrix}
		1 & 0  \\
		0 & -1
	\end{bmatrix}
\]

\section*{Rotation}
- Rotational Matrices rotate vectors counterclockwise by $\theta$:
\[
	\begin{bmatrix}
		\cos{\theta} & -\sin{\theta} \\
		\sin{\theta} & \cos{\theta}
	\end{bmatrix}
\]
- Applications:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Image processing rotations.
	\item Computer Graphics.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Scaling}
- Definition: Stretch or compress vectors.
- Applications:
\begin{itemize}
	\item Adjusting shapes in graphical designs.
	\item Uniform vs Non-uniform scaling.
\end{itemize}
